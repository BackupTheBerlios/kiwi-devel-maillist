> What do you mean by an initrd for re-booting the system? Could you please elaborate?

for images which store data persistently the task of mkinitrd was to
collect the data required for reboot and build a small initrd out of it.
Stuff like 'what is my root partition', 'which drivers needs to go in', etc, etc
the kiwi initrd is normally replaced by that mkinitrd call.

> Also, Could you please help me understand the need/use-case for creating an initrd image after booting to the live image?

for a live image this of course doesn't make sense. I was thinking on the
other image types which persistently store an initrd with the data required
for rebooting the system

> I wanted to point out the fact that the mkintrd tools/program will not be available in RHEL6 anymore. Dracut is the utility that is going to be available for building the initrd image. ok, so for any other than live images based on RHEL this needs to be
